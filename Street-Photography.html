Street Photography

Street photography celebrates the casualness of life and its passing. In those continuous streams of flesh and bones whizzing through the streets, there are those who run but also those who rest, each one a witness to the passing of time, each with their own life and identity.

In my photos, I immortalise the diversity and extravagance of those who populate and walk the city streets. Street photography is a hymn to life and portrays people from all strata, classes and social and geographical backgrounds. Trivial moments, such as a gentleman lying on a bench, can fit perfectly into urban geometry as a piece of a jigsaw puzzle. But it can also be that next to the bench there are three brothers playing together. One covers his eyes blinded by the sunlight, while the other two remain indifferent with their sunglasses. This slight difference may upset the composition. Elements that disrupt the constructed order or elements that align perfectly with its geometries portray the universality of the everyday.
