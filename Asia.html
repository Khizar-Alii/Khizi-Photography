My Asia

The Asian continent holds a special place in my heart, a place where my nostalgia is the most heartfelt and the most sincere.

From South-East Asia to South Asia, I tell of - beyond the exotic and the legends - a macro-continent with a thousand facets and scents, of witch doctors and fishermen, of beauty but also of poverty. And it is precisely here that the everyday, the ancient, the thousand-year-old nature, the lived and the human make themselves most felt. In my photographs, I speak of places where materialism has not yet permeated, where spirituality can be found in the street, as you walk: people who prostrate themselves before holy men to charge themselves with energy, temples where rats are considered gods, forests where spirits act as guardians. This world is a vaccine to the engulfing progress that with its box of illusions replaces idols, robes and temples in new models and lifestyles.
